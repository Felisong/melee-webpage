Super Smash Bros Melee is...

A crossover figthing game with classic Nintendo Characters of it's time. It released in 2001, developed by Hal Laboratory and published by Nintendo for the Gamecube. The game is the second instalment in the Super Smash Bros. series, following the original Super Smash Bros. for the Nintendo 64.

Melee distinguishes itself from other fighting games through it's unique gameplay mechanics. The damage system is based on percentage, and the higher percentage you get to, the farther your character can be knocked back by attacks. The goal is to knock opponents off the stage, rather than depleting their health. This goal forces players to focus on their positioning and edge guarding.

Melee features multiple game modes, both singe-player and multiplayer. The highlight to me is the competitive game mode, versus mode. In which two players face off with 4 stocks (lives) each. There are 25 playable fighters (26 if you consider Sheik and Zelda seperately). Although there is a tier list on how good or bad these characters are, competent players have proven time and time again that all characters can give a good fight.

Super Smash Bros. Melee has maintained an active and passionate competitive community since it has released. It has a high skill ceiling, allowing for advanced techniques and strategic play. The gameplay is fast-paced and technical, which later installments lose in return to make the games more party oriented.
